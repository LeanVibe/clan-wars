# Progression & Monetization Snapshot

Synthesised from `docs/raw/beta-launch-guide.md` for quick reference when designing progression hooks or economy features.

## Ranking Ladder
1. **Academy Student** – Tutorial complete → starter deck
2. **Genin Candidate** – 10 wins → Uncommon pack
3. **Genin** – 25 wins → Clan specialisation choice
4. **Experienced Genin** – 50 wins @ 60% WR → Rare selection
5. **Chunin** – 100 wins + tournament participation → Epic guarantee
6. **Elite Chunin** – 200 wins @ 65% WR → Clan master training
7. **Jonin** – 500 wins + regional top 16 → Legendary choice
8. **Elite Jonin** – 1000 wins @ 70% WR → Village representative cosmetics
9. **Kage Candidate** – Global top 8 → Exclusive leader card
10. **Kage** – Global champion → Season legendary creation

## Card Economy
- **Fusion**: 3× common → 1 uncommon, 3× uncommon → 1 rare, 3× rare → 1 epic, 2× epic → 1 legendary
- **Upgrade Stars**: +10% stats per star (1-5); Lv3 unlocks enhanced ability, Lv5 adds secondary ability
- **Deck Completion**: Target 30-card competitive deck via combination of fusion, quests, and rank rewards

## Free-to-Play Path
- Daily login: 1 common pack
- Quests: 3 wins → 1 uncommon card
- Rank-ups: Guarantee rare/epic/legendary depending on tier
- Weekly free tournaments with meaningful rewards
- Fusion converts duplicates into higher rarity (primary long-tail driver)

## Premium Enhancements (Time Savers)
- Card Packs: $0.99 basic, $4.99 premium, $19.99 legendary guarantee
- Season Pass: $9.99 (premium track, cosmetics, accelerated rewards)
- Direct Cards: Commons $0.10 → Legendaries $19.99 (ensure competitive integrity by mirroring F2P availability)
- Premium Tournaments: $4.99 entry with boosted prize pools
- Cosmetics: Animated cards, VFX, victory poses (no gameplay impact)

## Progression Timelines
- Week 1: Starter deck completed, reach Genin
- Month 1: Full clan collection, Chunin-ready
- Month 3: Multi-clan strategies unlocked, Chunin rank
- Month 6: Village mastery, Jonin contention
- Year 1: Complete collection achievable, Kage aspirant

Use these numbers when designing progression-related mechanics (e.g., reward tables, matchmaking tiers) to stay aligned with the original monetisation intent while preserving competitive fairness.
